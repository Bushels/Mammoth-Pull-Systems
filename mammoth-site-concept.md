# Mammoth Pull Systems Site Concept

## Direct recommendation

Build the site as a cinematic one-page product story with one clear interactive moment in the hero:

- the page opens in a stormy snowfield
- the user drags a tow line or handle to "pull" the Mammoth into frame
- that interaction reveals the brand promise and transitions into the two-model story

This is better than a normal industrial brochure site because the product is visually unusual, physically large, and built for harsh terrain. The homepage should feel like equipment entering a hostile landscape, not a generic manufacturer template.

## Brand decision to lock down first

- Standardize the company name everywhere: `Mammoth Pull Systems` or `Mammoth Pulling Systems`
- Standardize the 25' model spelling: `Mastadon` or `Mastodon`

If those names drift across the site, the brand will feel unfinished immediately.

## Visual thesis

Cold, heavy, engineered, remote. The site should feel like a steel machine moving through white silence.

## Content plan

1. Hero
   Mammoth Pull Systems, one-sentence promise, primary CTA, dominant image/video, interactive pull moment.
2. Support
   Explain what these sleds are for and why Alaska is the core use case.
3. Detail
   Split the product story into `Mastadon` and `Mammoth` with scale, dimensions, and use-case differences.
4. Final CTA
   Request specs, start a build inquiry, or book a call.

## Interaction thesis

Use only three motions:

- snow particles in the first viewport only
- drag-to-pull hero interaction that reveals the sled and headline
- scroll-linked scale reveal for the two sled sizes

Anything beyond that risks turning the page into gimmick software instead of heavy-equipment marketing.

## Recommended page structure

## 1. Hero: Pull Into Frame

Headline ideas:

- Built To Move Heavy Loads Across Snow And Ice
- The Heavy-Haul Sleds Built For Northern Terrain
- Mammoth Pull Systems

Support line:

- We build oversized pull sleds for remote transport across snow and ice, with Alaska as the clearest operating environment.

CTA ideas:

- View The Two Sleds
- Request Specs
- Start A Build Inquiry

Hero behavior:

- Background starts as a low-contrast arctic scene
- light snow falls in front of the scene
- a tow strap, cable, or handle sits near the lower third
- dragging it left-to-right pulls the sled silhouette or full image into frame
- once fully pulled, the logo and headline sharpen in and the page invites scroll

Why this works:

- it makes the user physically participate in the idea of hauling
- it communicates force, scale, and purpose without explaining too much
- it is memorable, but still simple enough to execute

## 2. Use Case: Why Alaska

Section headline:

- Built For Remote Winter Hauling

Points to communicate:

- snow and ice terrain
- remote access logistics
- hauling equipment, materials, and support loads
- large payload mindset over recreational sled language

This section should use one strong field image and short operational copy, not a grid of icons.

## 3. Product Story: Two Sleds, Two Scales

Layout idea:

- a horizontal or stacked comparison with oversized type for `25'` and `53'`
- the product names sit underneath as the second line
- each model gets one best photo, one short descriptor, and a small spec list

Suggested framing:

- `Mastadon`
  25-foot sled for smaller heavy-haul jobs, tighter access, or support logistics
- `Mammoth`
  53-foot sled for maximum scale, oversized transport, and serious remote hauling

Do not bury this in tabs. Let both products exist on the page at once so the size difference is obvious immediately.

## 4. Construction / Build Confidence

Section headline:

- Engineered For Abuse, Built For Work

Potential content:

- fabrication quality
- structural steel construction
- tie-down or deck options
- towing configuration
- custom build capability

This section should feel factual and mechanical. Good place for close-up images, weld details, deck details, or underside structure.

## 5. Proof / Gallery

Show:

- sleds in snow
- equipment or loads on deck
- scale references with trucks or people
- manufacturing details

Best format:

- one large lead image
- 4 to 6 secondary thumbnails
- optional short looping field clip if you have one

## 6. Inquiry CTA

Keep the end simple:

- Need a heavy-haul sled for snow or ice operations?
- Tell us your terrain, load, and target dimensions.

Form fields:

- name
- company
- location
- email
- what are you hauling
- expected terrain
- approximate size needed

## Visual system

## Color direction

- off-white snow tone
- near-black steel tone
- cold blue-gray as the main atmosphere
- one safety orange or amber accent for CTA states only

Avoid bright corporate blue. It will make the brand feel generic.

## Typography

- use one bold condensed or industrial display face for headings
- pair with a clean sans-serif for body copy

The type should feel fabricated, not luxury-minimal.

## Texture

- snow haze
- steel grain
- faint topographic or wind-swept linework

Use texture sparingly. The real product photography should do most of the work.

## Interaction notes

## Snow effect

- keep it subtle and only in the hero
- disable or reduce it on mobile and for reduced-motion users

`Reduced motion` is an accessibility setting that asks sites to minimize animation for people who are sensitive to movement.

## Drag interaction

- keep the drag distance short
- make it obvious that dragging does something
- after completion, allow a replay button but do not force it again

## Scroll behavior

- use sticky sections for the size comparison if the imagery is strong
- otherwise keep scroll conventional and fast

## Copy tone

The copy should sound like industrial equipment, not outdoor lifestyle branding.

Use language like:

- built
- hauled
- remote
- oversized
- snow and ice
- engineered
- fabricated
- load
- transport

Avoid language like:

- adventure
- explore
- freedom
- rugged lifestyle

## Simpler fallback if you want lower risk

If you want a cleaner first version, skip the drag interaction and do this instead:

- full-bleed hero image
- subtle snow
- large logo
- oversized `25'` and `53'` markers lower on the hero
- click to jump into each model section

This is the simpler and safer version to launch first. The drag-to-pull hero can be added later once the base site is working.

## Best next build path

1. Pick the final company name and product spellings.
2. Select the 3 best images:
   one hero image, one Mastadon image, one Mammoth image.
3. Confirm whether you want version A:
   launch-simple hero with snow only
   or version B:
   interactive drag-to-pull hero.
4. Build a one-page landing site before adding extra pages.
5. Add a PDF spec sheet later instead of overloading the homepage.
